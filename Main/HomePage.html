Weather forecasting is the process of predicting future atmospheric conditions using data from weather stations, satellites, radars, and other sources. Meteorologists analyze this data alongside numerical models to forecast short-term (up to a few days), medium-term (up to a week), and long-term (beyond a week) weather conditions. Methods like persistence, trend analysis, climatology, and numerical weather prediction (NWP) are employed, often aided by supercomputers and specialized software. Forecasts are crucial for public safety, agriculture, transportation, and energy management, helping prepare for and mitigate the impacts of weather events.
